The Dispatch

The Georgian Highway

Fog for breakfast. Stone towers built for siege. A mechanic who pours you coffee and calls it charity.

By the Editor Filed from Mestia Six minute read
42°43′25″N 42°44′12″E Elev. 1,400 m 02:14 CET Field-recorded

Mestia, GeorgiaThe air tasted of rusted iron and old snow. We crossed the valley before dawn. The headlights punched holes in the fog. The fog did not move. It resented us.

You do not end up in Svaneti by accident. You end up here because you made a sequence of poor decisions and committed to them. That is the whole trick, in travel and most other things.

On Architecture

The towers are the tell. Stone. Squat. Built to absorb arrows and opinions. Svaneti assumes history repeats. It assumes the next repetition involves your house. The assumption has been correct often enough to pay for itself.

A man in Ushguli once told me his great-grandfather had bricked up a window in 1922 and nobody had reopened it. I asked why. He looked at me the way you look at a child who has asked why the sky is blue. "Because," he said, "nobody has needed to."

Here you pay for the logistics. Flour up a mountain. Not the butter. A shepherd, near Adishi

A black coffee is free if you know the mechanic. About twelve lari if you don't. The croissant is a separate problem.

On Roads

The road worsened, the way roads do past a certain altitude. Concrete. Then gravel. Then theory. The rental agreement had assumed pavement. The rental agreement had been unread.

A cow stood in the middle of the track for a long time. We sat in the car and watched it. It watched us back. Nobody spoke. After a while it walked off. There was no larger lesson.

The map shows a track. Locals say it washed out in October. My last visit was 2018. The roads are a fraction better. The isolation is absolute. Cell service is a rumour. Wi-Fi is a philosophical concept. The only reliable signal is the one the mountains send every evening, when the clouds descend and the temperature drops twenty degrees in an hour: go inside, or become the story.

I went inside.
